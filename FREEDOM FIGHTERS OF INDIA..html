FREEDOM FIGHTERS OF INDIA

Honoring the legends of the Indian Independence Movement

Bhagat Singh

  • Regarded as one of the most influential revolutionaries of the movement.
  • Joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA).
  • Was deeply affected by the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre as a child.
  • Involved in the Lahore Conspiracy Case against British officer Saunders.
  • Exploded non-lethal smoke bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929.
  • Used his trial as a platform to spread the message of revolution.
  • Conducted a 116-day hunger strike in jail for prisoner rights.
  • Authored the famous essay 'Why I am an Atheist' while imprisoned.
  • Popularized the slogan 'Inquilab Zindabad' (Long Live the Revolution).
  • Was executed at the young age of 23, becoming a supreme martyr (Shaheed).
